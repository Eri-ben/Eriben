Toyota Material Handling
Design Review of I_Site

As part of my Summer Internship at Toyota Material Handling, I conducted a Design Review of a website service that was planned for re-design.

The background

When I joined Toyota Material Handling as a Summer Intern, the idea was that I would partake in an intern project group to develop a prototype for an in-house tool to facilitate the work process for the engineers of the company. After some researching into the topic, however, our team concluded that it would simply be more beneficial for the company to buy an existing solution, as the development of a completely new one (technically speaking) would cost much more in terms of prototyping and testing physical parts. For this reason, my background as a UX-Designer was less needed in this project, and I moved on to become more of a Business Analyst.

However, as I now had more time free, I contacted the ordinary UX Team at Toyota Material Handling and was given the task to perform an expert/design review of Toyota Material Handling's "I_Site" which had been on the UX Team's radar for redesign for a while.

The context

When I joined Toyota Material Handling as a Summer Intern, UX as a concept was still fairly new for most of the company and it still had some work to do in order to be fully embraced by the teams. Prior to hiring UX Designers, the design of the systems mostly landed on the hands of the programmers, which lended the developed products to have a high degree of feeling of ownership by those who had created the systems.
