About me

For as long as I can remember, I've had a lasting fascination about human use of technology and human behaviour in society. Even in my early years, I was drawn to naturally appearing trails in parks and forests. It was not their physical apperance that interested me, rather I realised how deep they really were, since for every path that had been procedurally created by us humans also hinted on a plethora of stories.

In a way, walkpaths are fossil not of what people are, but of what people do. I often pondered, and actually still ponder about, what people did to form these paths - and why they were eventually formed to begin with.

I'd like a piece of meaningful design that makes a difference. But some Swedish locourice would be fine too. Or kimchi, or ice-cream.

My interests and drives

My special interest and focus lays in understanding and designing for those environments that are really complex, and where flexible and sustainable communication needs to be witheld between multiple users at the same time. We live in an entangled and busy world, but our wiring is not done very well even these days. Products, services and so on don't talk with each other very well and this is especially true for collaborative matter. In the future, technology has to be sure to work in a society, and not only for a pack of individuals. I'm currently sketching on starting my own personal blog to discuss these topics as I find them very important, and I hope to open it one day very soon!

My aim as a UX practicioneer is to deliver meaningful design that either help users and stakeholders in their daily life, or at least gives them a fantastic experience they will remember. But sometimes, I'd just want people to not notice my design at all! That would just mean that the design (hopefully) does what it should do, and does not cause any trouble for the user. That would, in fact, be a fantastic result.
